The Basics

What Is an Empty Leg?

Every time a private jet is chartered for a one-way trip, the aircraft must travel back — or reposition to another location — without passengers. This flight is called an empty leg, deadhead, or ferry flight.

Rather than fly empty, operators list these slots with JetLux at heavily discounted rates. You get the exact same aircraft, crew, and level of service — at up to 75% below standard charter prices.

Timing is flexible within a window, but dates are generally fixed. Empty legs are ideal for travellers with flexible schedules or those departing near our listed airports.

Questions & Answers

Empty Leg FAQ

What is an empty leg flight?+

An empty leg occurs when a private jet must reposition to its next departure location without passengers. Rather than fly empty, operators list these routes with JetLux at 50–75% below standard charter rates — same aircraft, same crew, same luxury.

How much can I save on an empty leg?+

Discounts typically range from 50% to 75% below standard charter rates. Last-minute and longer-haul empty legs tend to offer the deepest savings. Each listing shows the original charter price alongside the empty leg rate.

Are empty leg flights safe?+

Yes. Every empty leg on JetLux operates on FAA and EASA-certified aircraft with fully qualified crews. Safety standards are identical to any full-price charter — the only difference is the price.

Can I change the date or time of an empty leg?+

Empty leg flights have fixed departure windows tied to the aircraft's schedule. Timing flexibility is limited, though small adjustments can sometimes be made. Our team will communicate all constraints clearly before you confirm.

How do I get notified about new empty leg deals?+

Sign up for JetLux Empty Leg Alerts above. Enter your preferred routes and aircraft type and we'll send you an instant notification the moment a matching flight is listed — before it sells out.
